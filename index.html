About Nepal

Geography

Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia with an area of 147,516 km², located between China and India. It contains 8 of the world's 10 highest mountains.

Culture

Nepal is a multicultural country with over 125 ethnic groups and 124 languages. The main religions are Hinduism and Buddhism.

Administration

Nepal is divided into 7 provinces, 77 districts, and 753 local units including 6 metropolitan cities, 11 sub-metropolitan cities, and 276 municipalities.

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Lumbini

Lumbini, Birthplace of Buddha

Sacred pilgrimage site where Siddhartha Gautama, the Lord Buddha, was born in 623 B.C.

Bhaktapur

Kathmandu Valley

Includes seven groups of monuments and buildings displaying the full range of historic and artistic achievements.

Chitwan

Chitwan National Park

Home to endangered species like the one-horned rhinoceros and the Bengal tiger.

Cultural Highlights

Dashain

The longest and most auspicious festival in the Nepalese annual calendar, celebrated for 15 days.

Tihar

The festival of lights, where crows, dogs, cows, and oxen are worshipped in successive days.

Holi

The festival of colors, celebrated with enthusiasm and the throwing of colored powder and water.

Dal Bhat

The staple meal consisting of lentil soup (dal), rice (bhat), vegetables, and pickles.

Momos

Tibetan-style dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, served with spicy tomato chutney.

Newari Cuisine

Unique flavors from the Newar community including dishes like Bara, Chatamari, and Yomari.

Daura Suruwal

Traditional Nepali male dress consisting of a knee-length shirt (daura) and tapered trousers (suruwal).

Gunyu Cholo

Traditional female dress with a blouse (cholo), skirt (gunyu), and shawl.

Newari Dress

Distinctive black sarees with red borders for women and tapalan for men in the Newar community.
